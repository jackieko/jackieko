What I Do

If I’m not avidly watching a UVA basketball game, here’s what else I’m doing on Grounds:

During the Spring semester, I volunteer as a Site Coordinator at Madison House’s Creating Assets, Savings, and Hopes (CASH) program. As a team we have helped file over 2,400 tax returns resulting in a direct economic impact of over $4.1 million dollars to the Charlottesville community.I have provided free tax return services for the 2015 and 2016 fiscal year to graduate students, local low-income Charlottesville residents, the elderly, and disabled persons.
As PocketPoint’s Marketing Representative, I promote the PocketPoints app on our university campus through class talks, tabling events, and social media posts. I was able to attain a goal of 100+ downloads within a bi-week of introducing the app on Grounds! This educational based app is perfect when we need a snack for late night study sessions so hop on over to their site and download. Catch me on Grounds to find out more about this app; I’d be happy to share more about it!
I am part of HackCville’s Wireframe program, where I have learned all the handy dandy skills to build this website you’re looking at right now! I learned how to code HTML/CSS and Javascript in a 12 week intensive course. I’m still brushing up on my coding but I plan to constantly learn and expand upon my skills. Through HackCville I was able to meet great people and learn an incredible amount of material (plus they also have a super cute clubhouse)!
